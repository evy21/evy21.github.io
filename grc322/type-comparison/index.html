Neue Haas Grotesk vs. Helvetica Neue

History of each typeface

Neue Haas Grotesk

Neue Haas Grotesk is a sans-serif typeface designed by Max Miedinger in 1957. The typeface was inspired by earlier grotesque typefaces from Germany and Britain. Miedinger's goal was to create a neutral, clear, and readable typeface that could be used for any kind of text. The typeface was influenced by Akzidenz-Grotesk from Berthold. became a symbol of Swiss technology and achieved immediate global success.

Helvetica Neue

Hlevetica Neue was designed to improve the original's legibility, structure, and weights. Max Miedinger and Eduard Hoffmann created the original Helvetica in Switzerland in the 1950s. The font was originally called "Neue Haas Grotesk" but was renamed "Helvetica" in 1960. Neue Helvetica has heavier punctuation marks, more spacing between numbers, and additional weights. it was designed to be more structurally unified, with improved legibility.
